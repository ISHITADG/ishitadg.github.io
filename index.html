Short-Form Video Understanding

My recent work focuses on understanding and analyzing short-form videos, which are characterized by unique editing techniques, narrative structures, and engagement patterns. Through papers like "SmartEdit" and "SKALD", I explore how AI systems can detect and understand editing-driven engagement factors, shot assembly patterns, and coherent multi-shot video creation.

This research directly informs the SVU 2025 workshop, where we're establishing benchmarks and exploring novel methods for understanding this rapidly growing class of visual content.

Multimodal Learning & Visual Storytelling

Through "VISIAR", I work on empowering Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) for visual story ideation, exploring how vision and language can be integrated to create compelling narratives. This research bridges the gap between understanding visual content and generating creative, engaging stories.

The focus is on developing systems that can understand the creative intent behind visual media and assist in storytelling workflows, particularly for short-form video content.

AI Systems for Creative Applications

My research spans from fundamental computer vision problems to practical applications in creative tools. I develop intelligent systems that can understand user intent, predict engagement, and provide meaningful assistance in video editing and content creation workflows.

This work is particularly relevant for platforms where creators need tools that understand the nuances of modern video content, from editing techniques to narrative structures that drive viewer engagement.
