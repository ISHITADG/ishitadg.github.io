SVU 2025 Workshop

The First Workshop on Short-Form Video Understanding

Workshop Highlights

  • • Exploring New Frontiers: This workshop brings together researchers working on understanding short-form videos, which represent 90% of internet traffic and have 2.5x higher engagement than longer videos.
  • • Key Topics: SV data collection and benchmarking, analysis and understanding techniques, detection of looped narratives and juxtaposed videos, and evaluation of engagement factors.
  • • ICCV 2025: Co-organizing this workshop at one of the premier computer vision conferences, featuring invited talks from leading researchers and accepted papers on cutting-edge short-form video understanding.

Data-Driven Workflows

My current research focuses on training models to understand data and using those insights to close the loop in data-driven workflows. I develop intelligent systems that can reflect on engagement patterns and determine optimal content styles and formats based on user requests and performance data.

This work is currently applied to videos in content creation workflows, where I explore how data-driven agents can create variations for different creative types, optimize content styles that resonate better with audiences, and generate variations while maintaining stylistic consistency. The goal is to enable closed-loop, data-informed decision-making in creative workflows.

Content Creation Applications

Through papers like "SmartEdit" and "SKALD", I apply data-driven approaches to content creation, exploring how models can understand editing patterns, engagement factors, and shot assembly to improve video creation workflows. These systems learn from data to optimize creative decisions.

This research directly informs the SVU 2025 workshop, where we're establishing benchmarks and exploring novel methods for understanding and optimizing short-form video content through data-driven approaches.

Multimodal Learning & Visual Storytelling

Through "VISIAR", I work on empowering Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) for visual story ideation, exploring how vision and language can be integrated to create compelling narratives. This research applies data-driven approaches to understand creative intent and optimize storytelling workflows.

The focus is on developing systems that can learn from data to understand creative patterns and assist in content creation workflows, with applications in video and visual storytelling.
